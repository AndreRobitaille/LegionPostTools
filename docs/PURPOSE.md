# Purpose

LegionPostTools exists to help American Legion posts run their internal work with better continuity, clearer records, and less manual document handling.

The first real installation is Robert E. Burns Post 165 in Two Rivers, Wisconsin. Post 165 currently relies on Word documents, PDFs, email threads, phone recordings, transcripts, and manual officer follow-up for work that should be easier to preserve and continue.

## Why This Matters

American Legion posts depend on volunteer officers and active members. Officers change. Committee work spans months. Meeting decisions need to be remembered. Minutes and agendas are not just paperwork; they are institutional memory.

The app should help preserve that memory without forcing officers to become software experts.

## What the App Should Respect

- American Legion ceremony and meeting rhythm.
- Robert's Rules style governance.
- Official minutes and record authenticity.
- Officer roles such as Commander and Adjutant.
- Committee and volunteer work that carries across meetings.
- The practical reality that many users are older or not confident with computers.

## AI's Role

AI can draft, summarize, organize, and suggest. It is not the authority.

Humans remain responsible for reviewing, approving, attesting, distributing, and accepting official records. The app should make that human responsibility clear in the workflow.

## What This Is Not

LegionPostTools is not generic nonprofit software. It is not a public website. It is not a SaaS platform today. It is an internal American Legion operations tool that may become installable by other posts or Legion-family installations.
