# Users and Context

This document preserves user context for developers and AI agents. It is not an end-user manual.

## First Installation

The first real installation is Robert E. Burns Post 165 in Two Rivers, Wisconsin.

Post 165 is a medium-sized American Legion post:

- About 121 members in good standing.
- About 20-25 members at a typical membership meeting.
- About 15 fairly active officers, committee participants, and volunteers.

Use this context to make grounded product decisions, but do not hard-code Post 165 assumptions into the application.

## Primary Early Users

### Commander

The Commander needs broad visibility, final review/approval of meeting records, and continuity across officers, committees, and recurring post business.

### Adjutant

The Adjutant is central to agendas, minutes, records, correspondence, and official documentation. The minutes workflow should treat Adjutant review and attestation as a core responsibility.

## Later Internal Users

### Officers

Officers may need to view records, submit reports, contribute agenda items, review minutes, or manage work related to their office.

### Committee Chairs and Committee Members

Committees and active volunteers may need to track ongoing items, add agenda context, and preserve decisions across meetings.

### Active Volunteers

Some important post work is done by members who are not formal officers. The app should not assume only elected officers matter.

## General Members

Most post members may not log into early versions. They may receive agendas, minutes, announcements, or records produced by the app.

## Computer Confidence

Many users may be older or uncomfortable with complex software. Product decisions should favor:

- Guided workflows.
- Clear labels.
- Few choices at once.
- Plain language.
- Forgiving draft/review states before official records become immutable.

Do not design for power users first.
